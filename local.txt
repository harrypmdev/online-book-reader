192ð“€´_OUR LIVES_
193ð“€´
194ð“€´Our lives are songs.  God writes the words,
195ð“€´  And we set them to music at pleasure;
196ð“€´And the song grows glad, or sweet, or sad,
197ð“€´  As we choose to fashion the measure.
198ð“€´
199ð“€´We must write the music, whatever the song,
200ð“€´  Whatever its rhyme, or metre;
201ð“€´And if it is sad, we can make it glad.
202ð“€´  Or if sweet, we can make it sweeter.
203ð“€´
204ð“€´One has a song that is free and strong;
205ð“€´  But the music he writes is minor;
206ð“€´And the sad, sad strain is replete with pain,
207ð“€´  And the singer becomes a repiner.
208ð“€´
209ð“€´And he thinks God gave him a dirge-like lay.
210ð“€´  Nor knows that the words are cheery;
211ð“€´And the song seems lonely and solemn--only
212ð“€´  Because the music is dreary.
213ð“€´
214ð“€´And the song of another has through the words
215ð“€´  An under current of sadness;
216ð“€´But he sets it to music of ringing chords,
217ð“€´  And makes it a pean of gladness.
218ð“€´
219ð“€´So, whether our songs are sad or not,
220ð“€´  We can give the world more pleasure.
221ð“€´And better ourselves, by setting the words
222ð“€´  To a glad, triumphant measure.
